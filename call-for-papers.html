Paper Submission Guidelines

Authors are invited to submit their original unpublished papers that demonstrate current research on one of the session topics of interest. Papers should be prepared in PDF form using the Springer LNCS/LNAI style (max 10 pages) and submitted via easychair with the following link:

https://easychair.org/conferences?conf=ismis2026

All submitted papers will be reviewed by ISMIS PC members.

Awards

ISMIS 2026 will award the best conference paper and the best student paper (in this case, the student must be the first author).

Publication of papers

The ISMIS'2026 proceedings will be published by Springer in Lecture Notes in Artificial Intelligence.

Special issue on JIIS Journal (Springer)

Authors of the best papers (up to 17) will be invited to submit their extended versions to the Special ISMIS 2026 Track of the Journal of Intelligent Information Systems (JIIS) published by Springer (https://link.springer.com/journal/10844)

Generative AI

If authors use any Generative AI model (e.g., ChatGPT, CLAUDE, LLaMA, or similar) at any stage of the paper-writing process, they assume full responsibility for all content, including checking for plagiarism and the correctness of the submission. We welcome papers that describe research on or involving LLMs. Any text generated by an LLM as part of the paper methodology or experimental analysis must be properly documented and described in the paper.
